About my city

Kanchipuram, also known as the “City of Thousand Temples”, is a historic and sacred city in Tamil Nadu, India. It is one of the seven Moksha-puris (sacred cities believed to lead to salvation in Hinduism). The city has been a great center of culture, learning, and spirituality since ancient times. Kanchipuram was once the capital of the Pallava dynasty and later flourished under the Cholas and Vijayanagara rulers, who built many grand temples showcasing Dravidian architecture. Some of the famous temples include Kailasanathar Temple, Ekambareswarar Temple, and Kamakshi Amman Temple, which attract pilgrims and tourists from all over the world. Apart from its religious importance, Kanchipuram is globally renowned for its silk sarees, often called Kanchipuram sarees, woven with pure mulberry silk and zari, making them one of the finest traditional garments in India. The city is also known for its contribution to philosophy, being home to great scholars like Adi Shankaracharya, who established his mutt there. Today, Kanchipuram stands as a blend of tradition, culture, spirituality, and craftsmanship, representing the rich heritage of Tamil Nadu.

Anderson Higher Secondary School in Kanchipuram is one of the oldest and most respected educational institutions in the district. Established during the colonial period by Christian missionaries, the school has a long history of providing quality education to students from different backgrounds. It is known not only for its academic standards but also for encouraging discipline, values, and extracurricular activities. Many students from this school have gone on to achieve success in various fields, carrying forward its legacy of excellence. The school’s campus, teachers, and traditions continue to inspire generations of learners in and around Kanchipuram.
